MASTER DESIGNER V3.0







About


MASTER DESIGNER V1.0 , presented by the Media Unit of SLTC, was a 4-day workshop series strictly focused on Photoshop, Illustrator, and InDesign tools. This was followed by MASTER DESIGNER V2.0 , an all-island competition designed to evaluate the most talented individuals in the field of graphic design across the nation.


Now, Master Designer V3.0 is here as the ultimate all-island graphic design competition and workshop series. Aimed at discovering top design talent in Sri Lanka, it promotes creativity and growth in digital design, including the use of AI tools. It consists of three immersive phases, from expert-led workshops to a live, physical final round.

GuideLines

Teams or individuals interested in participating in the competition should register through the registration link.
Everyone should fill out all the information in the registration form correctly.
Anyone above the age of 15 can participate in the competition.
The registration deadline varies for workshops and competition rounds. Please refer to the timeline for specific dates.
After registering your team, you can't change your team members.
Upon successful registration, participants will receive a confirmation email.
After the registration process, you will get a number. You should keep that number with you for the future work of the competition.
If you are participating individually, you cannot participate in a team.

Master Designer V2.0 Highlights

PHASE 01
In the first phase, designs for one field selected from the given fields are submitted under the online mode. During the submission, the contestants should submit a suitable name for the creation and the contestants can submit the design individually or as a team of three members. 15 teams and 15 individual competitors will be selected for the final stage of the competition that is the physical competition round.
PHASE 02
In the second phase (final phase) the two selected groups, 15 teams and 15 individual competitors, will measure their skills at a specific time. There will be one task for individual competitors and three tasks for group competitors. Among the designs created in this way, the greatest designs are evaluated.
